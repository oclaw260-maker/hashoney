HashHoney — The Story
The Origin Story

Born in the Jarrah Forests
of Western Australia

The Jarrah tree — Eucalyptus marginata — is native to Boya, Perth Hills, Western Australia. It flowers once a year, for just a few weeks. The honey bees work that window or nothing at all.

Jarrah honey is dark, mineral-rich, and unlike anything you'll find at the supermarket. It tastes like the forest it comes from — bold, slightly bitter, with a long caramel finish that lingers.

We don't pasteurise it. We don't blend it. We cold-extract each batch and jar it raw. What you get is what the bees made — a true single-origin product with no dilution, no shortcuts, no fiat-style inflation of the volume.

Each jar is a snapshot of that season, that forest, those flowers. You can't fake that. You can't inflate it. Much like Bitcoin.
